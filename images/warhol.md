# The "Flavor Dream" Master Template: Satellite Surrealism
### **The Andy Warhol Edition**

**Style:** A **silk-screen print aesthetic** inspired by **Andy Warhol's factory work**. Features high-contrast images, intentional "registration errors" (color bleeding outside lines), and a synthetic, neon palette.

**The Vista:** A vast aerial vista of **[City, Country]**, rendered from a satellite perspective as a high-contrast, two-tone silk-screened map. Landmarks like **[Landmark 1]** and **[Landmark 2]** are simplified into flat, celebrity-style icons.

**The Anomalies:**
* **Primary:** One colossal, city-sized **[User-Selected Primary Ingredient]** appears as a **3x3 Repeating Grid** of identical icons floating in the sky, each in a different jarring neon color combination (e.g., Electric Blue/Hot Pink). The "Life-Form" is a repeated, high-contrast silhouette interacting with the grid.
* **Secondary:** Large **[Remaining Ingredients]** drift at varying altitudes as repeated icons, suggesting an infinite assembly line.
* **Lighting & Shadows:** There are no natural shadows; immense scale is communicated by **flat color-offset blocks** that mimic massive printing errors falling over the city grid.

**The Palette (National-Culinary Sync):**
* The color scheme is a synthetic, neon, clashing blend of **[Ingredient Colors]** and **[National Colors]**.
* **Atmosphere:** The sky is composed of flat, offset silk-screen layers of vibrant, unnatural colors.
* **Geographic Feature:** Iconic features like **[River/Sea]** are simplified into high-contrast, two-tone silk-screened blocks.

**The Atmosphere:** Flying **[Local Vehicle Type]** move among the clouds as repetitive, stamped silhouettes with color registration errors. **[Texture/Botanical Elements]** drift like stamped commercial products.

**The Text:** The phrase **"[User-Selected Striking Phrase]"** is printed in a **stamped, repetitive font** across the sky, as if from a massive commercial rubber stamp.

**The Scale:** **Tiny, repetitively stamped silhouette figures and flat silhouette cars** must be visible on the ground, overwhelmed by the massive, mechanized commercial production above them.
