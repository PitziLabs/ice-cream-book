# Cardamom-Pistachio Kulfi

*India's frozen dessert tradition, now doable on a weeknight*

**Difficulty:** CHILL — No eggs, no churning. You’re warming milk, blooming saffron, grinding pistachios. That’s it. The only real skill is restraint—especially with the rose water.

**Total Time:** 7–9 hours (about 20 minutes active, the rest is freeze time doing its thing)

**Allergen Information:** Contains tree nuts (pistachios).

Kulfi is dense, rich, and built on concentrated milk flavor. Traditionally, you’d reduce milk for hours. That still rules.

This version skips that. Uses condensed and evaporated milk. Gets you almost all the way there with a fraction of the effort.

Same flavor profile. Same texture. Less standing over a pot.

No eggs. No churn required. Mix, flavor, freeze.

## Ingredients

**Base:**
- 2 cans (14 oz each) sweetened condensed milk
- 2 cans (12 oz each) evaporated milk
- 1 cup heavy cream
- 2 tbsp whole milk powder (optional but adds richness)

**Aromatics:**
- 8-10 green cardamom pods, lightly crushed
- Generous pinch of saffron threads (15-20 threads)
- 3 tbsp warm whole milk (for blooming saffron)

**Pistachio Elements:**
- 3/4 cup raw unsalted pistachios (preferably skinned)
- 3 tbsp whole milk (for grinding)

**Optional Rose Water Swirl:**
- 2 tbsp rose water (get the good stuff from an Indian grocery)
- 2 tbsp sweetened condensed milk
- Tiny pinch of ground cardamom

**For Serving:**
- Extra chopped pistachios
- Optional: edible rose petals, silver leaf (varak) if you're feeling fancy
- Optional: sliced mango or falooda noodles for traditional serving

## Instructions

**Bloom the Saffron (do this first):**

Lightly crush the saffron threads between your fingers. Drop into a small bowl with 3 tbsp warm milk. Stir and let sit for at least 10 minutes.

The milk should turn deep golden-orange. That’s your saffron milk.

**Prepare Pistachios:**

If your pistachios still have skins, blanch them: boil 1 minute, drain, then slip skins off by squeezing each nut. It’s tedious. Worth it.

If they’re already skinned, skip ahead.

Take about 1/2 cup pistachios and grind with 3 tbsp milk until completely smooth. No chunks. No grit. Set aside.

Coarsely chop the remaining 1/4 cup. Set aside separately.

**Make the Base:**

Pour condensed milk and evaporated milk into a large heavy-bottomed saucepan. Add cream and milk powder if using. Add crushed cardamom pods.

Whisk until smooth. Milk powder may clump at first—keep going, it’ll dissolve.

**Infuse Gently:**

Set over medium-low heat. You’re warming, not boiling.

Stir occasionally so nothing sticks. Heat until steaming with tiny bubbles at the edges (about 170–180°F). Around 8–10 minutes.

Reduce heat to low. Let it simmer very gently for 5 minutes, stirring occasionally.

This is about flavor, not reduction.

**Add the Flavor Bombs:**

Remove from heat. Remove and discard cardamom pods.

Add pistachio paste. Whisk until fully incorporated.

Add saffron milk, including threads. Stir well.

You should have a pale golden mixture that smells strongly of cardamom, saffron, and pistachios.

**Taste It:**

Taste while warm.

You’re looking for:
- Sweet but not overwhelming
- Strong cardamom presence
- Light saffron note
- Nutty pistachio

If cardamom is weak, add 1/2 tsp ground cardamom and stir.

Freezing dulls flavor. Aim slightly stronger than final.

**Strain and Cool:**

Strain through a fine-mesh sieve into a bowl.

Cool to room temperature, stirring occasionally so a skin doesn’t form.

Once cool, cover and refrigerate at least 4 hours or overnight. It will thicken.

**Optional Rose Water Swirl (make while base chills):**

Mix rose water, condensed milk, and a tiny pinch of ground cardamom until smooth.

Refrigerate.

**Important:** Rose water is strong. Start light if unsure.

**Freeze It:**

Stir chilled base to recombine.

Add chopped pistachios. Mix evenly.

**Traditional Method (Molds, No Churning):**

Pour into molds (kulfi molds, popsicle molds, or small cups).

If using rose swirl: layer kulfi mixture with small amounts of rose mixture and gently swirl. Don’t overmix.

If molds don’t have sticks, cover with foil and insert sticks after 2–3 hours when partially frozen.

Freeze 6–8 hours or overnight until solid.

**To unmold:** Run mold under warm water 10–15 seconds, then pull out.

**Ice Cream Maker Method (If You Want):**

Churn chilled base to soft-serve consistency.

Add chopped pistachios in the last minute.

If using rose swirl: layer and swirl in a container.

Freeze at least 4 hours to firm.

## Notes

**Shortcut Method:**

Traditional reduction takes hours. This doesn’t.

This is how a lot of home cooks do it now. It works.

**Why Condensed + Evaporated:**

- Condensed milk: sweetness, richness, smooth texture
- Evaporated milk: concentrated milk flavor without extra sugar
- Cream: fat, softness

Together: close to reduced milk without the time.

**Cardamom Matters:**

It defines the flavor. Use whole pods if possible.

Ground works, but add at the end.

**Saffron:**

Expensive, but you only need a pinch. Big impact on flavor and color.

Optional if needed, but you’ll miss it.

**Pistachios:**

Use raw, unsalted.

Alternatives:
- Almonds → Badami Kulfi
- Cashews → Kaju Kulfi
- No nuts → Malai Kulfi

All legit.

**Rose Water:**

Optional. Polarizing.

Use lightly or skip.

**Texture:**

Kulfi is dense. That’s intentional.

Small portions. Rich payoff.

**Milk Powder:**

Optional but useful. Adds body and creaminess.

**Molds:**

Use whatever works—kulfi molds, popsicle molds, cups, trays.

**Serving:**

Often paired with falooda, mango, or served plain.

**Storage:**

Keeps up to 2 weeks. Texture holds well.

**Why It Stays Smooth:**

High sugar + fat + milk solids = fewer ice crystals.

**Variations:**

Mango, paan, extra saffron, plain, chocolate, coffee.

Base method stays the same.

**Cultural Context:**

Originates from the Mughal era. Still widely made and eaten across South Asia.

The shortcut doesn’t dilute that—it keeps it practical.

## What it Tastes Like

Dense and rich.

Cardamom forward. Warm and slightly floral.

Saffron underneath, subtle but distinct.

Pistachios add nuttiness and texture.

If using rose: light floral note, not overpowering.

Sweet, creamy, and concentrated.
